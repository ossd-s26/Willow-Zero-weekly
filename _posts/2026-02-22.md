Project eval: pidgin

Pidgin is an open source multi-platform messenger that I use extensively for IRC, and occasionally hook up to my discord before remembering why I dont do that. Issue tracker is [here](https://issues.imfreedom.org/issues/PIDGIN?u=1), and seems relatively active, responses tend to come on the scale of a few days, which isnt as fast as it could be but is a fairly healthy response speed. The documentaion is highly confusing unfortunately, and is at [pidgin.im](https://pidgin.im/). There used to be a second, out of date copy of the documentation elsewhere, but I cant seem to find that one anymore - maybe they got rid of it finally. 

Its [blog](https://pidgin.im/post/) has a recent update, and of course, it has an IRC channel. 

its [repositoris](https://keep.imfreedom.org/pidgin/) have mostly not been updated in over a year, but builders, nest, and pidgin have all bee updated recently, on the scale of a few months or a few days. 

Pidgin is written in C, which I do have some grasp of. License is GPL2. 


Signal:

Signal is spread over multiple repositories. the one I'm most interested in is signal-android, as it is the client I am most likely to use in future - once I get it to work on my phone. 

[Issue tracker](https://github.com/signalapp/Signal-Android/issues), seems relatively active, with respones on the scale of weeks. Most recent commits are from last week, so the dev community is relatively active. 

The project as a whole has a [forum](https://community.signalusers.org/), which isnt the most active forum I've seen, but is definitely more alive than most. 

Signal-android is written in Java and Kotlin. I have experience with Java but not with Kotlin. Licence is AGLP3.



I rather like the more interview-style presentation that Linus Torvalds gave. I felt that the worried presented about AI and bad packages were well-articulated, but I feel there's a more fundamental issue with AI-generated code quality, and as more people use AI to generate their code, the models could devolve into creating truly bad code via self-seeding.
