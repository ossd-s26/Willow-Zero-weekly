## 1
I think projects can benefit a lot by putting their philosophies on interpersonal interaction down on paper. I have bee n in communities where people were known to be racist, or misogynistic, or just plain rude, but nothing could be done to encourage change because there was no formal social contract to enforce. 

Go replaces the 'enforcement' section with a 'conflict resolution' one. They probably made this change to make the conflicts arising from violations of the community guidelines less hostile, and to create a more collaborative atmosphere, even when someone messes up. 

[Eclipse Code of Conduct](https://www.eclipse.org/org/documents/Community_Code_of_Conduct.php)

The Eclipse version adds "actions", "no retaliation", and "amendments" clauses, I suspect due to either their particular history with the code of conduct, or their nature as a more rigid project, focused on one program, rather than a whole language.
